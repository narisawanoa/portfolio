The Structure

October 2025 / Steel
I explored my physical presence in this world through creating this structure. This sculpture is an extention of my physicality and its system.
3.5 × 4 × 3 ft

Plot for the plotter

September 2025 / Multimedia Installation
I made a walking track for the plotter, generating a walking path in real time. I added several variables to track the walk, such as distance in pixels, duration time in seconds, motor usage, and the density of lines. I used white pencil on white paper to focus viewers on the internal algorithms of the plotter for it to walk.

In communication with an unknown Entity

May 2025 / Multimedia Installation
An interactive software where the viewer could reflect responses to carry out conversation with the forest. I translated the software into physical installation by making an object that can receive signals from the classroom and responds to them.

No more understanding of the world (Live recording and projection of the sky)

April 2025 / Multimedia Installation
A live projection of the sky recording on site. The sky is divided by invisible borders that can easily be trespassed. Yet, I am bounded on the land unable to see the other skies but my own.

Mission Statement

March 2025 / Multimedia Installation
A site-specific installation for RISD beach. The mission statement of RISD administration is played on repeat from a speaker inside an enclosed cube, filtered by the cube and the traffic noise surrounding the institution.

Light in the colony

May 2025 / Wire, rice paper, reed
In continuation from the hole in the colony sculpture, I put a light source inside the colonial structure/architecture to cast light and shadow through the transparency of the rice paper. Similar to the previous sculpture, the rice paper was used to stretch over and connect the reed structures and create holes that could allow the light to pass through.
40 × 3 × 45 in

Hole in the colony

April 2025 / Wire, rice paper, reed, wood
From the architectural structures that I observed in NYC, I analyzed their form and scaffolding to create a sculpture that represents a living colony gathering in the architecture. The rice paper used to stretch and connect the linear reed structures dries out and begins to crack after some time, creating a nervousness and tension around it.
40 × 35 × 30 in

Tide

March 2025 / Bristol paper
Paper construction of water from 2D to 3D.

Head

June 2024 / Clay
Self Portrait. I carved through the surface of clay to remember my own face. My interest in sculpture began with the sense of touch and creation of space that allowed me to see myself and my own experience inside it. This is a self-portrait in three-dimensional space, capturing my experience of carving out my own head.
